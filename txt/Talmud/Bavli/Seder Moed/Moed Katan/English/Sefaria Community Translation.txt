The Rabbis taught:
From when do we turn over the beds?
Our Rabbis taught: Formerly, they were wont to serve drinks in a house of mourning, the rich in
white glass vessels and the poor in coloured glass, and the poor felt shamed: they instituted therefore
that all should serve drinks in coloured glass, out of deference to the poor. Formerly they were
wont to uncover the face of the rich and cover the face of the poor, because their faces turned livid in
years of drought and the poor felt shamed; they therefore instituted that everybody's face should be
covered, out of deference for the poor.38 Formerly. they were wont to bring out the rich [for burial]
on a dargesh39 aid the poor
